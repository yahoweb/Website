04 — Editorial

Oldboy

Translating Park Chan-wook's Oldboy into a script-based publication — where typography and layout become active participants in the film's psychological tension.

Type Editorial
Year 2025
Role Editorial Designer
Source Oldboy (2003)
Scope Publication · Typography
Tools Photoshop · Illustrator · InDesign
Status Academic Project

Overview

Before a film reaches the screen, it exists as a structure of scripts, dialogue, and scenes. This project reconstructs Oldboy by translating the finished film into a script-based publication — enabling readers to navigate scenes and dialogue in parallel and reinterpret the narrative through a new reading experience.

The publication mirrors cinematic pacing through typography, spacing, and layout — guiding readers through moments of tension, silence, and emotional intensity.

Approach

Script excerpts and visual references are aligned in parallel, creating a layered reading experience that echoes the film's narrative rhythm. By reframing a finished film as a publication, the book transforms viewing into reading — encouraging active interpretation through typography, sequencing, and spatial rhythm.

Balancing urgency with restraint, the palette uses crimson red to signal tension, deep charcoal to ground the narrative with cinematic weight, and soft porcelain to maintain clarity and editorial readability.
