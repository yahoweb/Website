02 — Campaign

Tatox

Reframing tattooing from stigma to identity — a campaign that repositions the marked body as a site of cultural expression and subcultural belonging.

Type Campaign
Year 2025
Role Art Director
Client Tatox (Concept)
Scope Identity · Packaging · Social
Tools Photoshop · Illustrator
Status Academic Project

Overview

In many Asian societies, tattoos have long been stigmatized — associated with deviance rather than self-expression. TATOX challenges this narrative by reframing tattooing as a form of identity, cultural belonging, and subcultural pride.

Inspired by the fluid movement of ink, the TATOX logotype embodies the tattoo as a living mark — personal and cultural, temporary in motion yet permanent in meaning. From stigma to identity. From taboo to tattoo.

Approach

The palette — warm beige, muted indigo, stone gray — balances human warmth with quiet strength. It avoids the aggressive aesthetics often associated with tattoo culture, instead drawing from the materials and skin tones that make each mark intimate and irreplaceable.

Packaging, social content, and sticker systems carry the campaign across physical and digital touchpoints, giving the brand a presence that feels as considered as the art it champions.
